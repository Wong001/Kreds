Why this hierarchy
Filter is the function The everyday job of the circle is choosing whom to read — so its everyday form is a chip bar that costs 40 pixels. The radial map is the expanded state: orientation, onboarding, and brand, summoned when wanted.
People are destinations Two navigation axes: Journal moves through time, Circle moves through people. Click a person anywhere — chip, avatar, node — and their profile opens in place. On the phone the circle is the launcher; the destinations of Kreds are people, not pages.
Receipts, not promises Click a post's scope pill: Kreds shows which devices actually hold it, because scope here is physics — encrypted to keys, existing on named machines. That's the one thing a server platform can't draw. Friend-lists they have; receipts they can't.
